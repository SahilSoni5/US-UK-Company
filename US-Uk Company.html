Market Context & Drivers

India's economy is on a strong growth trajectory, with its GDP exceeding £3 trillion and projected to grow at 5% annually. This economic expansion is creating a massive consumer market, expected to be the world's second-largest by 2030. The burgeoning middle class and rising disposable incomes are fueling demand for global brands, transforming India from a manufacturing hub into a primary consumer market. This shift requires foreign companies to invest heavily in localized brand building and consumer-facing marketing to capture significant market share.

The India-UK FTA is a landmark deal set to boost bilateral trade by $34 billion. It significantly reduces tariffs on UK products, making them more competitive. Duties on key exports like Scotch whisky will drop from 150% to 40% over time, and luxury car duties will be slashed from over 100% to 10% under a quota system. This creates a highly favorable environment for UK brands in sectors like Food & Drink, Automotive, and Consumer Goods to expand aggressively, supported by increased marketing investment budgets due to lower operational costs.

The US-India trade relationship is robust but complex, marked by a recent 25% tariff on Indian imports. This challenges companies like Apple, which are pursuing a "China Plus One" strategy to diversify manufacturing and make India a major export hub. Despite tariff headwinds, the strategic imperative to de-risk supply chains from China keeps US investment strong, particularly in electronics manufacturing. US companies must therefore adopt agile PR strategies to manage narratives around supply chain shifts and focus marketing efforts on India's vast domestic market.
